# Memory: Brian's Plumbing Works / Plumbing-Geek

## The Vision
Create a distinctive, trustworthy website for Brian's Plumbing Works, doing business as Plumbing-Geek, positioned as a Portland, Oregon plumbing contractor with a rare specialty in vintage plumbing restoration. The site should feel like a master craftsperson's workshop crossed with a technical field guide: deeply knowledgeable, practical, a little old-school, and clearly more specialized than a generic plumbing company.

The business should read as the right choice for two overlapping audiences:
- Homeowners who need an experienced plumber they can trust for repairs, upgrades, and code-conscious work.
- Owners of older homes, restorers, preservation-minded clients, and detail-oriented DIY researchers looking for expertise in vintage plumbing systems, restoration decisions, and historically sensitive work.

The final experience should elevate the brand beyond a basic contractor brochure. It should communicate authority, decades of hard-won expertise, Portland-local relevance, and a strong point of view about how plumbing should be done.

## Persona & Tone
### Brand personality
- Expert but grounded.
- Opinionated in a useful way.
- Scholarly without sounding academic.
- Old-house friendly.
- Precise, honest, and unflashy.
- Craftsman energy, not corporate energy.

### Writing voice
Use first-person or close-to-founder language selectively, especially in About and educational sections, to preserve the reference site's human, hands-on authority. The tone should feel like an experienced plumber explaining the right way to do something after years in the field.

### Tone attributes
- Direct
- Instructive
- Credible
- Patient
- No hype
- No trendy startup language
- No generic luxury-service cliches

### Messaging style
- Prefer concrete claims over vague marketing.
- Explain why something matters, not just what is offered.
- Use service language that signals skill and judgment.
- When discussing restoration, stress preservation, compatibility, code realities, repair-vs-replace judgment, and respect for original fixtures/materials.

## Design Identity
### Visual direction
Build a custom visual system inspired by old plumbing manuals, utility diagrams, machine-shop materials, and restored period hardware.

### Mood
- Copper
- Brass
- Porcelain
- Cast iron
- Paper schematics
- Aged enamel signage
- Workshop orderliness

### Recommended palette
- Deep navy or blueprint ink
- Oxidized copper / verdigris accent
- Warm brass / antique gold accent
- Off-white / drafting paper background
- Charcoal / cast-iron text and UI support

### Typography
Avoid generic SaaS typography. Pair an expressive serif or industrial slab for headings with a highly readable body face. The system should feel intentional, sturdy, and a little archival.

### UI character
- Clean but not sterile.
- Use linework, sectional dividers, diagram-inspired rules, or subtle blueprint grids.
- Include visual references to pipe routing, fittings, restoration details, and technical annotations.
- Keep the site fast and modern, but do not flatten the personality into a standard contractor template.

## Comparative Research Directives
### Reference Site 1
Visit: `http://www.plumbing-geek.com/`

Use your own tools to inspect the live site directly. Extract and interpret the following:

#### What to capture
- The site's core title/meta positioning. A third-party summary indicates the site presents itself around helping with home plumbing projects and DIY plumbing guidance.
- The educational tone and structure.
- The founder-expert voice: practical, detailed, technical, independent.
- The types of subject matter covered, especially pages involving plumbing vents, drain tools, washing machine pipes, and similarly instructional content.
- Any signals that the business is rooted in problem-solving, code literacy, and long-form explanations rather than generic service marketing.
- Any original diagrams, process photos, plumbing part images, or illustrative assets that appear proprietary and relevant.

#### What to extract structurally
- How the site organizes knowledge topics.
- The density and cadence of instructional writing.
- The way the author explains cause-and-effect in plumbing systems.
- The balance between business identity and educational reference material.

#### What to adapt, not copy blindly
- The instructional authority.
- The specificity of the explanations.
- The sense that the person behind the site truly understands the underlying mechanics.
- The niche identity implied by the word "Geek."

#### What to modernize
- Replace any dated or cluttered presentation with a refined, high-contrast, mobile-friendly interface.
- Distill long-form educational material into scannable sections, cards, and clear calls to action.
- Preserve authenticity while improving hierarchy, readability, image presentation, and conversion flow.

#### What to avoid
- Do not recreate a dated web-1.0 layout.
- Do not reduce the brand to generic plumbing-stock visuals.
- Do not erase the technical personality in pursuit of sleekness.
